Information of choose part

Hair:

Hair is the thin strands that grow from your skin, mainly on your head, but also on other parts of your body.

Ears:

These help you hear. They catch sound waves and send them to your brain to make sense of sounds.

Cheeks:

Cheeks are made up of skin, fat, muscles, and bones. The cheekbone (also called the zygomatic bone) gives shape to your cheeks.

Mouth:

This helps you eat, talk, and breathe. It includes the tongue (for tasting and speaking) and teeth (for chewing food)

Lip:

You have two lipsâ€”the upper lip and the lower lip. They meet at the mouth and help in speaking, eating, and making facial expressions.

Chin:

The chin is the bottom part of your face, under your mouth. It helps with talking, eating, and gives your face shape.

Jaw:

The jaw is the lower part of your face that moves when you talk and chew. It includes the upper jaw (fixed) and the lower jaw (moves). The jaw holds your teeth and helps shape your face.

Nose:

The nose is the part of your face used for smelling and breathing. It has two openings called nostrils and filters the air to trap dust and germs. The nose also helps warm and moisten the air you breathe.

Eye::

The eye is the organ that allows you to see. It has a pupil that lets light in, surrounded by the colored iris. The lens focuses light onto the retina, which sends signals to the brain to create images. Eyelids and eyelashes protect the eyes.

Eyebrow:

The eyebrow is the strip of hair above each eye. It helps keep sweat out of your eyes and plays a role in facial expressions. Eyebrows vary in shape and thickness among people.

Forehead:

The forehead is the area above your eyebrows and below your hairline. It helps express emotions through wrinkles and gives shape to your face.
